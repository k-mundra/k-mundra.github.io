Background

I'm a second-year MS student at Georgia Tech's School of Computational Science & Engineering, where I work on physics-informed machine learning and inverse problems.

My current research focuses on building latent-space inverse solvers for Navier-Stokes flows. This entails inferring initial conditions from final-state observations using ML surrogates and global optimization. Before this, I developed multifidelity neural surrogate models using a Kennedy-O'Hagan autoregressive framework extended with neural networks.

This summer I'm at Boom Supersonic as an Applied ML Engineering intern, building surrogate models for 3D turbomachinery compressor CFD by evaluating multiple SciML architectures.

I'm also a Contrary Research Fellow (1 of ~12 from ~300 applicants), where I analyze frontier deep tech companies.

Research

Latent-Space Inverse Solver for Navier-Stokes Flows
Jan 2026 – Present
Pestourie Group · Georgia Tech CSE
Developing a ViT-VAE with 8-dimensional latent space to infer initial conditions from final-state flow observations. The pipeline combines a neural surrogate with global optimization to solve the inverse problem without adjoint gradients. Training data generated with Dedalus.
Inverse Problems ViT-VAE Navier-Stokes Bayesian Optimization Dedalus PyTorch
Multifidelity Neural Surrogate Modeling
Aug 2024 – Feb 2026
Aerospace Computational Engineering Lab · Dr. Elizabeth Qian
Developed multifidelity neural network models using a novel Kennedy-O'Hagan autoregressive framework extended with neural networks, achieving up to 90% reduction in generalization error versus single-fidelity baseline. Validated on short column and Forrester benchmarks. PURA Salary Research Grant recipient; presented at Georgia Scientific Computing Symposium (poster) and GT Undergraduate Research Symposium (talk).
Multifidelity Kennedy-O'Hagan Neural Surrogates UQ JAX

Digital Twin for Data Center Cooling
