Background

I'm a first-year MS/PhD student at Georgia Tech's School of Computational Science & Engineering, where I work on physics-informed machine learning and inverse problems under my advisor in the Pestourie Group.

My current research focuses on building latent-space inverse solvers for Navier-Stokes flows — inferring initial conditions from final-state observations using ML surrogates and optimization. Before this, I developed multifidelity neural surrogate models using a Kennedy-O'Hagan autoregressive framework extended with neural networks.

This summer I'm at Boom Supersonic as an Applied ML Engineering intern, building surrogate models for 3D turbomachinery compressor CFD — evaluating POD+DNN, transformer-based, and physics-informed architectures for rapid design space exploration.

// 02 — research

Research

Latent-Space Inverse Solver for Navier-Stokes Flows
Jan 2026 – Present
Pestourie Group · Georgia Tech CSE
Developing a ViT-VAE with 8-dimensional latent space to infer initial conditions from final-state flow observations. The pipeline combines a neural surrogate (val PSNR 43 dB) with global optimization — CRS2 and Bayesian optimization via Ax — to solve the inverse problem without adjoint gradients. Training data generated with Dedalus at Re=5000; spectral low-pass filtering for GRF initial condition generation.
Inverse Problems ViT-VAE Navier-Stokes Bayesian Optimization Dedalus PyTorch
ML Surrogates for 3D Turbomachinery CFD
Jun 2026 – Aug 2026
Boom Supersonic · Applied ML Engineering Intern
R&D on scientific ML surrogate architectures for 3D compressor flow prediction, evaluating POD+DNN, transformer-based, and physics-informed models across accuracy and data efficiency tradeoffs. Goal: replace expensive high-fidelity CFD simulations to enable rapid design space exploration for supersonic propulsion systems.
Surrogate Modeling CFD POD+DNN Transformers Turbomachinery
Multifidelity Neural Surrogate Modeling
Aug 2024 – Feb 2026
Aerospace Computational Engineering Lab · Dr. Elizabeth Qian
Developed and tuned multifidelity neural network models using a novel Kennedy-O'Hagan autoregressive framework extended with neural networks, validated on short column and Forrester benchmarks. Achieved up to 90% reduction in generalization error versus single-fidelity baseline. PURA Salary Research Grant recipient; presented at two symposia.
Multifidelity Kennedy-O'Hagan Neural Surrogates UQ JAX
