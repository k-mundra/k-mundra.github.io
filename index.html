about

Background

I'm a first-year MS student at Georgia Tech's School of Computational Science & Engineering, where I work on physics-informed machine learning and inverse problems.

My current research focuses on building latent-space inverse solvers for Navier-Stokes flows — inferring initial conditions from final-state observations using ML surrogates and global optimization. Before this, I developed multifidelity neural surrogate models using a Kennedy-O'Hagan autoregressive framework extended with neural networks.

This summer I'm at Boom Supersonic as an Applied ML Engineering intern, building surrogate models for 3D turbomachinery compressor CFD by evaluating multiple SciML architectures.

I'm also a Contrary Research Fellow (1 of ~12 from ~300 applicants), where I analyze frontier deep tech companies.
